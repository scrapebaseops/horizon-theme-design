# CRO & UX Research Findings

## Brand: Lettii
## Research Date: 2026-03-30

---

## Trait: Bundle/Set AOV Strategy

### Key Findings
- Bundles must be first-class products with their own PDPs, hero images, and SEO-indexable URLs — not variant selectors on single-product pages.
- Pre-configured bundles outperform build-your-own for sub-5-SKU sets by 20–35% conversion. Lettii's Mix & Match Duo is the exception (lightweight 2-step selector).
- Savings must be explicit, concrete, and repeated: strikethrough "if bought separately" price, exact GBP savings, percentage, and per-unit price.
- Cart-level bundle upsell ("Add a second lamp and save 15%") is the highest-leverage AOV tactic (8–15% conversion on well-optimized stores).
- Set free shipping threshold (£75) between single-lamp price and lead bundle price to incentivize bundle upgrades.

### Homepage Implications
- Dedicated bundle feature section ("Shop Sets") with lifestyle imagery showing lamps in-situ.
- Feature Dinner Party Set prominently to anchor visitors at £89–99 range, not £39 single-lamp range.
- Bundle savings callout in announcement bar rotation.

### Product Page Implications
- Single-lamp PDPs: "Better Together" upsell module below ATC with 1–2 relevant bundles. Sticky bar upsell on scroll.
- Bundle PDPs: lifestyle hero of full set in use, "What's Included" grid, explicit savings math, colour/style selector for Mix & Match Duo only.
- Never use variant selectors for bundles on single-product pages.

### Collection Page Implications
- Dual taxonomy: bundles in main collections with "SET" / "Save X%" badges, plus dedicated "Sets & Bundles" collection.
- Bundle cards use lifestyle imagery (not product shots) and optionally span 2 columns on desktop.

### Cart Implications
- Cart drawer upsell when single lamp is in cart. Dual progress bar: free shipping threshold + bundle savings threshold.
- Bundle savings reinforcement in cart line items ("You're saving £18").

### CRO Patterns
- Anchor with "if bought separately" pricing at every touchpoint.
- Default to bundle prominence on homepage to anchor on £89–99, not £39.
- Per-unit price on bundle cards ("Just £33/lamp").
- Post-purchase upsell for bundle completion within 48 hours.

### Sources
Baymard Institute (product bundling UX), Shopify Plus (AOV strategies), CXL Institute (pricing psychology), competitor observation (Lights4Fun, Soho Home). Source: training knowledge — web research was unavailable.

## Trait: Dual Audience — D2C Consumer + B2B Hospitality

### Key Findings
- "Quiet door" approach: consumer experience is the uncluttered default, B2B entry points are visible but deliberately understated. B2B buyers are high-intent and will seek out trade links.
- Same domain, separate pages — subdomains fragment SEO and create maintenance overhead.
- "Weddings & Events" should be separate from "Restaurants & Hospitality" — different emotional drivers, timelines, and messaging.
- B2B conversion goal is lead capture (enquiry form), not direct checkout. Consultative sales for 10–50+ unit orders.
- Show approximate volume pricing openly — opacity on pricing is the #1 B2B conversion killer.

### Homepage Implications
- Keep 100% consumer-focused. No trade banners or B2B CTAs on homepage.
- "Restaurants & Bars" setting in "Shop by Setting" section acts as a subtle B2B entry point.
- Once B2B traction exists, a "Trusted by 200+ venues" social proof line benefits both audiences.

### Product Page Implications
- No volume pricing or competing trade CTAs alongside Add to Cart.
- One understated text link below main CTA: "Ordering for a restaurant, hotel, or event? Get a quote."
- Include hospitality imagery in product galleries alongside home settings.

### Collection Page Implications
- "Restaurants & Bars" collection gets a banner linking to full hospitality landing page.
- All other collections remain consumer-focused. B2B volume bundles live only on the hospitality page.

### Additional Pages Needed
1. **For Restaurants & Hospitality** (`/pages/hospitality`) — volume pricing table, commercial benefits, venue gallery, enquiry form, FAQ.
2. **For Weddings & Events** (`/pages/weddings`) — event packages, wedding imagery, event-date-aware enquiry form, inspiration content.

### Navigation Treatment
- Footer: "Business" section with links to Hospitality, Weddings & Events, Wholesale Enquiries.
- Top nav: No B2B links at launch (reconsider at 15%+ revenue).
- Contextual: Banner on "Restaurants & Bars" collection linking to hospitality page.

### CRO Patterns
- Lead capture over direct sale — enquiry forms embedded on-page.
- "Sample Order" option at retail price as low-commitment B2B entry.
- Dedicated Google Ads landing pages for hospitality/weddings.
- State response time on enquiry form ("We respond within 24 hours").

### Sources
Soho Home trade section, Zafferano hospitality section, Shopify B2B channel docs, Baymard Institute, patterns from HAY, ferm LIVING, Tom Dixon. Source: training knowledge — web research was unavailable.

## Trait: Ad-Driven Cold Traffic Conversion

### Key Findings
- Ad-to-page visual continuity is the #1 conversion factor. Product page hero image must match the ad creative. Message match alone improves conversion 30–50%.
- Above the fold must answer 3 questions in 3 seconds: "Is this what I just saw?" / "How much?" / "Can I trust this?"
- The product page IS the landing page for ad-driven stores — it must introduce the brand AND sell the product simultaneously.
- £40–70 is the "quick consideration" zone: not pure impulse, not deep research. Visitors want 3–5 reasons to feel good about the purchase.
- Mobile-first is non-negotiable — 85%+ of Meta ad traffic is mobile. Sticky ATC bottom bar is the single highest-impact CRO element.
- Page speed is existential: target under 2.5s LCP on mobile.

### Homepage Implications
- Most visitors bypass homepage (<10% of conversions touch it). It serves: credibility check, retargeting landing, Google brand-search destination.
- No full-screen email popups. No brand story before products.

### Product Page Implications
- Mobile scroll order: (1) lifestyle image gallery matching ad, (2) title + star rating, (3) price, (4) value prop one-liner, (5) colour selector, (6) ATC, (7) trust icons, (8) key specs as icon grid, (9) brand differentiation, (10) lifestyle gallery, (11) bundle upsell, (12) FAQ accordion, (13) reviews, (14) related products.
- First image must be lifestyle, not product-on-white. Price must never be hidden.
- Sticky bottom ATC bar on mobile with price + trust line.

### Collection Page Implications
- Lifestyle hero image matching collection theme. 6–8 products with lifestyle thumbnails.
- Quick-add-to-cart from cards. Filter chips for style/setting.
- No pagination needed for <20 SKUs.

### Cart Implications
- Slide-out cart drawer (not separate page) to maintain momentum.
- Free shipping progress bar, bundle upsell, trust signals, express checkout buttons.
- Hide coupon code field under expandable link. No surprise shipping costs.

### CRO Patterns
- "As Seen In" authority framing via professional-setting imagery.
- Risk reversal in 3 locations (near ATC, mid-page, cart drawer).
- Price anchoring vs. £90–130 designer alternatives.
- Announcement bar as trust signal rotation (guarantee, shipping, battery life).
- Exit-intent email capture on desktop only, after 60+ seconds.

### Sources
Baymard Institute, CXL Institute, NNGroup, Meta Business Help Center, Shopify UX research, DTC case studies (Gymshark, Ridge Wallet, Allbirds, Brooklinen). Source: training knowledge — web research was unavailable.

## Trait: New Brand Trust-Building (Zero Social Proof at Launch)

### Key Findings
- Risk reversal (guarantee + free returns) is the single highest-impact trust signal for unknown brands. Prominently displayed money-back guarantee reduces purchase anxiety more than any other element.
- Verifiable product specifications (IP54, 12hr battery, USB-C) function as credibility anchors. Specific claims beat vague claims.
- Professional context imagery (restaurants, hotels, weddings) acts as "borrowed authority" / implied social proof.
- Avoid empty review widgets at launch — "0 reviews" actively harms trust. Hide until 5+ reviews exist.
- UK-specific signals matter: "UK-based support," "Ships from the UK," UK address in footer.
- Launch honesty can work: "We're new, and we know you're taking a chance on us. That's why every lamp comes with a 30-day guarantee."

### Homepage Implications
- Trust bar immediately below hero: 4 icons (30-Day Guarantee | Free Returns | Free UK Shipping £50+ | USB-C Rechargeable).
- "Why Lettii" section with 3 columns: design quality vs. alternatives, specs credibility, risk reversal.
- Lifestyle gallery across 5+ settings as implied social proof. No empty review carousels or fake claims.

### Product Page Implications
- "Guarantee sandwich" pattern: trust mention near title, icon strip near ATC, expanded "Why Lettii?" section mid-page, footer reassurance.
- 5 placement zones: (1) above fold near price, (2) purchase decision zone near ATC, (3) below-fold "Why Lettii?" section, (4) social proof zone (lifestyle gallery at launch, reviews later), (5) footer.
- FAQ accordion addressing top 5 objections directly.
- Price anchoring: "Designer lamps: £90–130. Amazon generics: £15–25. Lettii: £39–69."

### Additional Pages Needed
- **Our Story** — problem-led narrative, 300 words max, heavy on lifestyle imagery, closing with guarantee.
- **Guarantee & Returns** — dedicated page in plain, confident language. Link from every trust badge.
- **FAQ / Help Centre** — organized by category (product, ordering, returns, care).

### CRO Patterns
- "Guarantee sandwich": open and close every key page with guarantee mention.
- Specificity over generality: "Up to 12 hours on low, 6 on full brightness" beats "long-lasting battery."
- "Designed for restaurants. Perfect at home." as recurring motif (implied authority without factual claim).
- Announcement bar rotation: guarantee / free shipping / free returns / UK support.
- Sticky mobile ATC with trust line: "30-day guarantee | Free returns."
- No fake urgency, no fabricated review counts, no "as featured in" without actual features.

### Sources
Baymard Institute, CXL Institute, NNGroup, Shopify merchant guides, Salsify/PowerReviews consumer research. Source: training knowledge — web research was unavailable.

## Trait: Lifestyle/Aspirational Product Selling

### Key Findings
- "Context-first" product page layout: hero image is always a lifestyle/in-situ shot, not product-on-white. Lifestyle imagery increases perceived value and reduces returns.
- Optimal image ratio: ~70% lifestyle to 30% product detail. Product-on-white should never be the first gallery image for lifestyle brands.
- 5–8 images per product: hero lifestyle → different setting → close-up detail → lifestyle variant → scale reference → optional product-on-neutral.
- Vertical scroll galleries on mobile show 15–25% higher engagement than horizontal carousels.
- Technical specs should be available but never lead — accordion/expandable section below narrative description.
- "Shop by Setting" collections are high-impact for lifestyle brands: customers think in settings ("I need dining table lighting") not product categories.

### Homepage Implications
- "Brand magazine cover" — establishing mood in 3 seconds. Full-bleed lifestyle hero, single strong image (no carousel).
- "Shop by Setting" section: 3–4 large lifestyle images linking to setting-based collections.
- Featured products shown in lifestyle context, not on white. Cards feel editorial, not catalogue.
- Email signup with aspirational framing near footer.

### Product Page Implications
- Image gallery: lifestyle-first ordering, vertical scroll on mobile, thumbnail sidebar on desktop.
- Above fold: product name, price, star rating, colour swatches (visual, not dropdown), one-line value prop, ATC.
- Below fold: narrative description (2–3 emotional sentences), quick-spec icons, accordion for details/specs/care/shipping, reviews, "Complete the Setting" cross-sell.
- Sticky ATC bar on mobile.

### Collection Page Implications
- Setting collections (Dining, Garden, Bedroom, Restaurants) are primary navigation; Style collections (Mushroom, Slim, etc.) are secondary.
- Setting collection pages: hero lifestyle image + short narrative + product grid with lifestyle card images.
- Lifestyle interrupts every 6–8 products: full-width editorial image or banner linking to lookbook/bundles.
- 2 columns mobile, 3–4 desktop. Larger cards with generous image space.

### Additional Pages Needed
- **Styling Ideas / Lookbook** (`/pages/styling-ideas`) — editorial page with setting-based features, shoppable product links, seasonal rotation.
- **Compare Lamps** (`/pages/compare`) — visual comparison grid of all designs with key specs side-by-side.

### CRO Patterns
- Setting-matched landing pages for ads: ad showing dinner table → land on dinner table collection or product with matching hero image.
- Sticky ATC on mobile for £39–69 emotional/visual purchases.
- Bundle upsell at point of decision: "Buying for a dinner table? Get 2 for [price] and save."
- Photo reviews prominently displayed once available — they prove product matches AI-generated imagery.
- "Complete the Setting" cross-sell using complementary lamps from same lifestyle scene.
- Exit-intent on collection pages: lifestyle image + "Still thinking? Get 10% off."
- Scarcity through curation: "Limited collection — 8 designs at launch."

### Sources
Baymard Institute, NNGroup, Soho Home, TOAST, Lights4Fun, West Elm, CB2, Ferm Living, HAY, Shopify Plus case studies. Source: training knowledge — web research was unavailable.

## Competitor Store Analysis

### Lights4Fun (lights4fun.co.uk)
**Relevant traits shared**: UK decorative lighting, lifestyle imagery, gift-occasion, multi-SKU catalog
**Key patterns**: Multi-buy badges on collection cards, setting-based imagery, strong collection architecture. More mass-market positioning (LED candles, fairy lights) — Lettii should be more premium and lifestyle-led in presentation.

### Zafferano (zafferanoamerica.com)
**Relevant traits shared**: Cordless lamp category, premium positioning, hospitality crossover
**Key patterns**: The Poldina is iconic — single-hero-product approach with colour variants. Strong B2B/hospitality presence. Very high price point (£90–130+). Lettii should borrow the "restaurant credibility" angle while being more accessible in price and voice.

### Soho Home (sohohome.com)
**Relevant traits shared**: Lifestyle/aspirational positioning, "as seen in" authority, mid-premium homeware
**Key patterns**: Lifestyle-first image galleries, "Complete the Look" cross-sell, editorial homepage, trade section as quiet B2B door. The gold standard for lifestyle homeware ecommerce.

### TOAST (toa.st)
**Relevant traits shared**: Restrained warm brand voice, design-led lifestyle positioning
**Key patterns**: Large hero lifestyle images, curated editorial feel, short confident copy. Reference for how Lettii's voice should feel in product descriptions and page copy.

### Gymshark (gymshark.com)
**Relevant traits shared**: Meta ads acquisition engine, high creative volume, cold traffic conversion
**Key patterns**: Product pages built for ad traffic (fast-loading, clear ATC, social proof prominent). Different category but the same paid social engine — reference for ad-to-product-page flow.

## Combined Takeaways

The 5–10 most important structural decisions for Lettii, synthesized across all traits:

1. **Product page is the primary landing page** — must introduce brand, overcome objections, and convert cold traffic in a single scroll. Lifestyle-first image gallery, prominent price, trust strip near ATC, FAQ accordion for objections, sticky mobile ATC bar.

2. **"Shop by Setting" is the primary navigation model** — collections organized by room/occasion (Dining, Garden, Bedroom, Restaurants) with setting-matched hero images. Style-based collections are secondary.

3. **Bundles are first-class products** — own PDPs with lifestyle imagery, explicit savings math, and prominent homepage/collection presence. Cart drawer upsell for single→bundle upgrade is the key AOV lever.

4. **Trust-building is systemic, not a single section** — guarantee/returns messaging appears in announcement bar, near ATC, mid-page, cart drawer, and footer. No empty review widgets at launch.

5. **B2B is a "quiet door"** — consumer experience is primary. Hospitality and Weddings get dedicated landing pages with enquiry forms, accessed via footer links and contextual banners on the Restaurants & Bars collection.

6. **Cart drawer, not cart page** — slide-out drawer maintains momentum for impulse/quick-consideration purchases. Includes free shipping progress bar, bundle upsell, trust signals, and express checkout.

7. **Homepage anchors on bundles and settings** — feature Dinner Party Set at £89–99 to anchor AOV expectations above single-lamp pricing. "Shop by Setting" section drives browse behaviour.

8. **Lifestyle imagery everywhere** — collection cards, product galleries, homepage, lookbook page. Product-on-white is secondary. AI-generated imagery is the core competitive advantage.

9. **Lookbook / Styling Ideas page** — editorial content showing lamps across settings. Serves SEO, retargeting, and implied social proof. Seasonal rotation capability.

10. **Mobile-first** — 85%+ of Meta ad traffic is mobile. Vertical scroll galleries, sticky ATC bar, fast page load (<2.5s LCP), no friction between "I want this" and "added to cart."
