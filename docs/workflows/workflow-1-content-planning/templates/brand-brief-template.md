# Brand Brief

Fill out this document as thoroughly as possible before starting Workflow 1. This is the **sole source of context** the agent uses to make content, structure, copy, and CRO decisions — there is no live website to reference. Anything left blank or vague will force the agent to make assumptions, which may not match your intent.

This brief is focused on the **brand, product, customer, and buying experience** — not on design or technical implementation. Visual direction and technical specs come later in Workflow 2.

Fields marked **(required)** must be filled in. Fields marked **(recommended)** significantly improve output quality. Fields marked **(if applicable)** can be skipped if they don't apply.

## 3. The Customer

**Primary customer** (required):
[Who is the main buyer? Be specific — demographics AND psychographics. E.g., "Women 28–45, skincare-aware, willing to spend more for clinically-backed products, researches ingredients before buying, follows skincare influencers, currently using 3–4 products from different brands and frustrated by the lack of results."]

**Secondary customer(s)** (if applicable):
[Any other important customer segments? E.g., "Gift buyers — partners or parents shopping for someone else. They need clear guidance on what to buy."]

**What problem are they solving?** (required):
[Why is the customer looking for this product? What pain point, desire, or need brings them to the store?]

**What objections do they have?** (required):
[What hesitations would prevent them from buying? List the top 3–5 objections. E.g.:
- "Is this actually different from what I've tried before?"
- "Will it work for MY skin type?"
- "It's expensive — is it worth it?"
- "What if I don't like it? Can I return it?"]

**How do they discover the brand?** (recommended):
[How do customers typically find the store? E.g., "Primarily Instagram and TikTok, some Google search for specific skin concerns, word of mouth." This influences which pages matter most and what content strategy to prioritize.]

## 4. The Competition

**Direct competitors** (required):
[Name 2–5 competitors and briefly say how this brand is different from each. E.g.:
- "The Ordinary — cheaper but overwhelming product range, no guidance"
- "Drunk Elephant — similar price point but more lifestyle-focused, we're more clinical"
- "CeraVe — mass market, clinical but impersonal, we're premium with better ingredients"]

**What makes this brand different?** (required):
[The competitive advantage in 2–3 sentences. This becomes core messaging throughout the site. E.g., "We're the only brand that formulates specifically for the barrier-damaged skin that results from over-exfoliation. Every product is tested on compromised skin, not healthy skin."]

**Aspirational brands** (recommended):
[Brands whose store *experience* you admire, even if they're in a different category. Be specific about WHAT you admire — the copy, the buying flow, how they explain their product, etc. E.g., "We love how Glossier makes skincare feel approachable, how Athletic Greens uses a single long-form page to sell one product, how Ritual shows exactly what's in each capsule."]
