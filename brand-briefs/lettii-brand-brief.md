## 1. The Brand

**Brand name** (required):
Lettii

**Tagline or slogan** (if applicable):
"Light the moment."

**Brand story** (required):
Lettii was born from a simple frustration: beautiful spaces shouldn't depend on wiring, cables, or candle wax dripping onto the tablecloth. We noticed that the warm, intimate glow you see in the best restaurants, boutique hotels, and styled wedding venues was almost impossible to recreate at home — or if you could, it meant fiddling with real candles, ugly battery packs, or cheap plastic lights that killed the mood. Lettii makes premium cordless table lamps that bring that effortless, high-end atmosphere to any setting — your dining table, your garden, your bedside, your next dinner party — with a single touch. No cords, no mess, no compromise on how it looks.

**Brand voice** (required):
- **Warm but not twee** — we talk like a stylish friend recommending something they love, not a homeware catalogue. No "cosy vibes" clichés.
- **Confident but understated** — we know the product looks and feels premium. We don't need to oversell it. The imagery does the heavy lifting.
- **Design-literate but accessible** — we reference interiors and styling without being pretentious. Someone who shops at Zara Home or browses Pinterest for table settings should feel at home.
- **Direct and concise** — short sentences. No filler. We respect people's time and attention span, especially in ads.

**Voice examples** (recommended):
- "One lamp. Every setting. Restaurant to bedside to garden party."
- "Touch to dim. USB-C to charge. That's it."
- "We made the lamp you've seen in every boutique hotel — and made it yours."
- "No wires. No wax. No reason not to."

**Brand values** (recommended):
- **Atmosphere over specs** — we sell the feeling, not the lumens. Every product decision and piece of creative starts with "does this make a space feel better?"
- **Design quality at an accessible price** — premium aesthetics without the designer markup. We believe great lighting shouldn't cost £150+.
- **Versatility** — one lamp, many lives. Home, hospitality, events, outdoors. We design for flexibility, not single-use.
- **Simplicity** — in the product (touch control, USB-C, done), in the buying experience (no confusing variants), and in the brand (clean, clear, confident).

## 2. The Product

**What does the store sell?** (required):
Premium cordless rechargeable LED table lamps. Each lamp features capacitive touch control, stepless dimming, USB-C charging, and an IP54-rated waterproof build suitable for both indoor and outdoor use. The range spans multiple design styles — from minimal mushroom silhouettes and slim Poldina-style columns to sculptural organic shapes, geometric neo-deco pieces, and crystal/glass accents — all in a curated colour palette led by black and gold (the two highest-demand colours by UK search volume).

**Catalog size and shape** (required):
Launch catalogue of approximately 20 SKUs — 20 distinct lamp designs across 5–7 style families. Initially available in 1–2 colourways each (primarily black and gold). The range will expand based on ad test performance — designs that convert well in Meta ads get reordered in larger quantities and additional colourways.

**Product collections / categories** (required):
- All Lamps
- Best Sellers (populated post-launch based on sales data)
- New Arrivals
- Shop by Style: Mushroom & Dome / Slim & Modern / Lantern & Shade / Sculptural / Geometric / Crystal & Glass
- Shop by Setting: Dining & Entertaining / Bedroom & Bedside / Garden & Outdoor / Restaurants & Bars
- Bundles & Sets
- Gifts

**Hero product(s)** (if applicable):
To be determined by the £1,000 Meta ad test. The launch strategy is to run 20 designs through paid ads simultaneously, identify the 3–5 top converters, then double down on those as hero SKUs. The likely hero will be a mushroom or slim column style in black — these are the most searched and most proven designs across competitors.

**Product variants** (if applicable):
Colour (Black, Gold, Silver/Chrome, White — starting with Black and Gold). Some designs may offer a warm white vs warm/cool adjustable colour temperature option. No size variants at launch.

**Price range** (required):
£39–£69 for individual lamps. £89–£149 for bundles/sets of 2–3 lamps. Positioning is mid-premium: above Amazon generics (£15–25) but well below designer brands like Zafferano (£90–130 per lamp).

**Bundles or kits** (if applicable):
- **Dinner Party Set** — 2 or 3 matching lamps at a ~15% discount vs buying individually
- **Mix & Match Duo** — any 2 lamps for a bundled price (encourages exploration)
- **Entertainer Pack** — 4 lamps (ideal for a long table or outdoor setting), deepest discount tier
- Bundles are a core part of the AOV strategy. Target AOV is £55–80, and bundles push the average order well above single-lamp pricing.

**Subscription / recurring purchase** (if applicable):
Not applicable at launch. May explore a "seasonal drop" model later — curated new designs released quarterly — but this is not a subscription business.

**Gift cards** (if applicable):
Yes. £25, £50, £75, and £100 denominations. Positioned for birthdays, housewarmings, and wedding gifts.

## 3. The Customer

**Primary customer** (required):
Women aged 28–45 in the UK, style-conscious, active on Instagram and Pinterest, who care about how their home looks and feels. They browse interiors content, save tablescaping ideas, and notice the lighting in restaurants and hotels. They're the person who sets the table properly when friends come for dinner, who lights candles for a Tuesday evening, who wants their outdoor space to feel like a Mediterranean terrace. Household income is mid-to-upper — not price-insensitive, but willing to spend £40–70 on something that elevates their space if it looks and feels worth it. They've probably bought cheap LED candles or fairy lights before and been disappointed by how plasticky or dim they looked.

**Secondary customer(s)** (if applicable):
1. **Hospitality buyers** — restaurant owners, bar managers, wedding planners, event stylists, boutique hotel operators looking for rechargeable table lighting that looks premium without the fire risk or running cost of real candles. They buy 10–50+ units at a time. (B2B audience, reached via separate landing page and Google Ads.)
2. **Gift buyers** — partners, parents, or friends buying for someone who "has everything" or who just moved into a new place. They need clear guidance on what to buy and benefit from gift wrapping / gift card options.
3. **Men 30–50** buying for a partner or for their own home/man cave/garden setup. Smaller segment but present, especially for the more architectural or geometric designs.

**What problem are they solving?** (required):
They want their space to feel warm, inviting, and stylish — especially when hosting — but real candles are messy, a fire risk, and annoying to manage. Wired lamps limit where you can place lighting and look cluttered. Cheap battery lights look terrible. They're looking for something that gives them "restaurant lighting at home" without any hassle — something they can set on the table, tap on, and forget about. For hospitality buyers, the problem is operational: they need consistent, safe, rechargeable table lighting that looks premium, charges easily, and survives daily use.

**What objections do they have?** (required):
1. "Will it actually look premium in person, or will it look cheap and plasticky like every other LED light I've bought?"
2. "How long does the battery actually last? I don't want it dying mid-dinner party."
3. "I've never heard of this brand — can I trust the quality?"
4. "Is it bright enough to actually make a difference, or just a dim novelty?"
5. "Can I use it outdoors? What about rain?"
6. "The price is more than I'd spend on Amazon — what makes this worth it?"

**How do they discover the brand?** (recommended):
Primarily through paid Meta ads (Instagram and Facebook) featuring AI-generated lifestyle imagery showing the lamps in aspirational settings — dinner parties, restaurants, garden terraces, bedside tables, weddings. Secondary discovery via Google Shopping and Google Search ads targeting terms like "cordless table lamp," "rechargeable table lamp," "restaurant table lamp," and "outdoor table lamp rechargeable." Some organic discovery via Instagram and Pinterest as the brand's content library grows.

## 4. The Competition

**Direct competitors** (required):
- **Lights4Fun** (lights4fun.co.uk) — £23.5M revenue, dominant UK player in decorative lighting. Strong product range (160+ SKUs) and retail distribution (M&S, John Lewis, Amazon). However, they focus on LED candles and fairy lights, not cordless table lamps. Their lifestyle imagery is home-focused only — zero hospitality, wedding, or restaurant scenes. Slow creative cycles, traditional photography only, no AI-generated imagery.
- **Auraglow** (auraglow.co.uk) — UK brand selling LED lighting including some rechargeable options. Lower price positioning, more utilitarian/functional branding, weaker lifestyle imagery. Less of a direct competitor on design quality.
- **Zafferano** (zafferanoamerica.com / UK stockists) — Italian brand, the "Poldina" is essentially the category-defining product for premium cordless table lamps. Beautiful design, strong hospitality presence, but very expensive (£90–130+ per lamp). Lettii can offer a similar aesthetic at half the price.
- **Amazon generics** — dozens of unbranded or white-label cordless lamps at £15–25. Low quality, no brand, terrible imagery, but they set price expectations at the bottom of the market. We need to clearly justify the price gap.
- **Humble Lights / Neoz / Imagilights** — premium/trade-focused cordless lamp brands. Strong in hospitality but minimal D2C presence, high price points (£100+), limited style range. Not competing on creative or brand experience.

**What makes this brand different?** (required):
Lettii is the only UK D2C brand combining premium cordless lamp design with AI-powered creative at scale. Competitors either sell expensive designer lamps with minimal marketing (Zafferano, Neoz), or sell cheap generic lights with no brand (Amazon). Lettii sits in the gap: genuinely good-looking lamps at £39–69, marketed through high-volume lifestyle imagery that shows customers exactly what their space could look like — across more settings, more styles, and more occasions than any competitor can produce with traditional photography.

**Aspirational brands** (recommended):
- **Zafferano** — for product design credibility and how they've made a single lamp silhouette iconic. We admire how the Poldina is instantly recognisable.
- **Fleux (flightclub.com-style experience brands)** — for how they make simple products feel like lifestyle purchases through imagery and editorial content.
- **TOAST (toa.st)** — for their restrained, warm brand voice and how they sell an aesthetic/lifestyle, not just products. Their photography makes simple things feel special.
- **Soho Home** — for how they leverage the "as seen in Soho House" angle to sell homeware at a premium. We want "as seen in the best restaurants" to work the same way for Lettii.
- **Gymshark** (early-stage) — for their Meta ads playbook: high creative volume, rapid testing, scaling winners aggressively. Different category, same paid social engine.

## 5. The Buying Experience

**Primary conversion goal** (required):
Buy a lamp (or lamp bundle) directly from a Meta/Google ad click-through. The most common conversion is: see ad → land on product page → add to cart → checkout.

**Secondary conversion goals** (recommended):
1. Add a second lamp or upgrade to a bundle (upsell at cart/checkout to increase AOV)
2. Sign up for the email list (pre-launch and for retargeting non-converters)
3. Browse the collection (for visitors not ready to buy from a single ad — let them find "their" lamp)
4. Follow on Instagram (brand building and retargeting pool)

**Path to purchase** (required):
1. **Ad → Product Page → Buy** (primary, ~60% of conversions): Customer sees a Meta ad showing a lamp in a beautiful setting. Taps through to the product page. Sees price, reviews, battery life, more lifestyle shots. Adds to cart. Checkout.
2. **Ad → Collection Page → Product Page → Buy** (~25%): Customer sees an ad that resonates with the setting but wants to browse more styles. Lands on a collection page (e.g., "Dining & Entertaining" or "All Lamps"), browses 4–6 options, picks one, adds to cart.
3. **Google Search → Product Page → Buy** (~15%): Customer searches "cordless table lamp UK" or "rechargeable table lamp." Clicks a Shopping ad or text ad. Lands on a product page. Compares with other search results. Buys if the product page is compelling enough.

**Key trust signals** (required):
- Customer reviews (target 50+ reviews within first 3 months via post-purchase email flows; display star rating prominently)
- "As seen in" lifestyle imagery — showing lamps in restaurant, hotel, and wedding settings creates implied professional endorsement
- 30-day money-back guarantee, free returns, no questions asked
- Free UK shipping on orders over £50 (or all orders, depending on margin analysis)
- USB-C charging (signals modernity and quality vs micro-USB competitors)
- IP54 waterproof rating (specific, verifiable claim)
- Battery life stated clearly (e.g., "up to 12 hours on a single charge")
- "Trusted by 500+ restaurants and venues" (once B2B traction is established)
- Secure checkout badges
- UK-based customer support

**Urgency / scarcity tactics** (if applicable):
- Low stock alerts on fast-selling designs (real inventory data, not fake)
- Seasonal collections with limited runs (e.g., a summer garden collection that won't be restocked)
- Launch discount for email subscribers (10–15% off first order)
- We deliberately avoid fake countdown timers, "only 2 left!" manipulation, or any tactic that feels dishonest. The brand should feel confident, not desperate.

## 6. Content & Messaging

**Key messages** (required):
1. "Restaurant lighting, at home" — the single most important message. Every ad and product page should communicate this transformation.
2. "One touch. Infinite settings." — touch to turn on, stepless dimming, colour temperature control. The product is simple and flexible.
3. "No cords. No wax. No compromise." — cordless, rechargeable, beautiful design. You don't have to choose between convenience and aesthetics.
4. "Charges like your phone" — USB-C, fast charge, 10–12 hours per charge. Modern, familiar, zero friction.
5. "30-day guarantee. Love it or send it back." — risk reversal, especially important for a new brand.

**Homepage intent** (required):
Establish the brand as a premium-but-accessible UK lighting brand. Lead with a hero lifestyle image (dinner table setting) and the core message ("Restaurant lighting, at home"). Showcase 3–4 best-selling lamps. Communicate trust signals (guarantee, reviews, shipping). Drive visitors to either the hero product, the full collection, or a "Shop by Setting" page. The homepage should feel like a beautiful interiors magazine, not a product catalogue.

**Content types planned** (recommended):
- [x] Lookbook / editorial imagery — AI-generated lifestyle scenes across all key settings (dining, bedroom, garden, restaurants, weddings, bars, hotels). This is the core content engine.
- [x] Video content — short-form (6–15 second) "place and glow" transformation videos for Reels and Stories. Before/after scenes. Product unboxing and first impression clips.
- [x] User-generated content / customer photos — post-purchase email requesting photos; repost customer images on social. Critical for social proof.
- [x] Email newsletter — value prop: "First access to new designs, styling ideas, and subscriber-only discounts." Segmented flows: welcome series, post-purchase, browse abandonment, seasonal campaigns.
- [x] Blog articles — SEO content targeting "how to style a dinner table," "best cordless lamps for restaurants," "outdoor dining lighting ideas," "wedding table lighting." Long-tail content play.
- [ ] Quiz or personalisation tool — potentially a "Find your lamp" quiz later (by room, style, occasion), but not at launch.

**Specific pages you know you need** (if applicable):
- "For Restaurants & Hospitality" — dedicated B2B landing page with volume pricing, testimonials, and a wholesale enquiry form
- "For Weddings & Events" — landing page showing lamps in wedding/event settings, with a "plan your event lighting" enquiry option
- "Our Story" — short, visual brand story page (why Lettii exists, what we stand for)
- "Styling Ideas" — lookbook-style page showing lamps in different real-world settings, doubling as inspiration and SEO content
- Size / spec comparison page — simple visual comparison of all lamp designs side by side with key specs (height, battery life, weight, colours)

## 7. Policies

**Shipping** (required):
Free standard UK shipping on all orders over £50 (2–4 business days via Royal Mail or Evri). Orders under £50: £3.95 standard shipping. Express next-day delivery available for £5.95. UK only at launch — no international shipping initially.

**Returns & exchanges** (required):
30-day money-back guarantee. Free return shipping for any reason — no questions asked. Full refund processed within 5 business days of receiving the return. Exchanges available for different designs/colours. Faulty items replaced immediately with free return label provided.

**Customer support channels** (recommended):
Email support (target response within 12 hours during business days). Live chat during UK business hours (9am–6pm Mon–Fri). Instagram DMs monitored daily. Comprehensive FAQ page covering charging, battery life, waterproofing, care instructions, and returns process.

## 8. Anything Else

**Additional context** (optional):

**Launch strategy:** The brand launches with a £1,000 Meta ad test campaign. 20 lamp designs are sourced in small quantities (approximately 10 units each) from Alibaba suppliers (Jimbu / Jianbiao Digital Trade) at $8–10 per unit. Each design is tested with AI-generated lifestyle ad creative across multiple settings. The top 3–5 performing designs become the core collection, reordered in larger quantities at better unit costs from the factory arm (Jianbian Lighting Technology) once MOQs of 500–1,000 are viable.

**Seasonal timing:** Launching into UK spring/summer 2026 is ideal — garden and outdoor entertaining demand peaks April–August. Wedding season runs May–September. Christmas gifting push from October. The product is year-round (bedroom, dining, hospitality) but outdoor/entertaining gives the strongest initial acquisition angle.

**AI imagery as a core capability:** The brand's creative pipeline is built around AI-generated lifestyle photography. This allows us to produce dozens of ad variations per lamp (same lamp in different rooms, settings, colour grading, seasons) at a fraction of the cost and time of traditional shoots. This is the operational advantage: we can test more creative, in more settings, faster than any competitor — and scale the winners immediately.

**Colour strategy:** Black and gold are the two launch colourways based on UK search keyword volume analysis. Black is the highest-volume search colour for cordless table lamps; gold is the strongest premium/aspirational colour. Silver/chrome and white are planned for the second production run based on demand signals.

**Platform:** Shopify. Paid acquisition via Meta Ads Manager and Google Ads. Email via Klaviyo. Analytics via Shopify + Meta Pixel + GA4.
