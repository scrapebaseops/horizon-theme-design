## 3. The Customer

**Primary customer** (required):
Gay men aged 22–45 who bottom (receptive anal intercourse) and want a more reliable, less burdensome prep routine. They're health-conscious, body-aware, and either currently using psyllium in another form (Metamucil powder, Pure for Men capsules, generic psyllium husk) or have tried and abandoned fibre due to gas, bloating, or inconvenience. They're active on TikTok and Instagram, follow queer health creators, and are comfortable discussing sexual health openly. Many are on PrEP (HIV prevention medication), indicating an already health-integrated lifestyle. They value products that speak to them directly rather than making them decode generic "digestive wellness" marketing for their specific use case. Willing to pay a premium over generic psyllium but price-aware enough to question Pure for Men's markup.

**Secondary customer(s)** (if applicable):
- **First-time bottoms / anxious experimenters** — Younger (18–25), research-heavy, anxiety-driven. They've read Reddit threads and TikTok guides and are looking for a product that validates their experience and makes the process less intimidating. They need reassurance and education more than anything.
- **Partners buying as gifts** — Tops or versatile partners who want to support their partner's comfort and confidence. Need clear, non-awkward product explanation and gifting-friendly packaging/messaging.
- **Pragmatic cost-optimisers** — Currently using Metamucil or generic psyllium to save money, but would switch to a gummy format if the price premium is reasonable and the product actually speaks to their use case.

**What problem are they solving?** (required):
The core problem is anxiety — not digestion. They're buying fibre to eliminate the fear of an accident during sex, to stop monitoring their body for disaster mid-encounter, and to reclaim sexual spontaneity. Pre-fibre, bottoming requires 3–6 hours of douching prep, turning sex into a scheduled event. Fibre replaces that with a daily gummy habit that makes them "ready" without planning. The physical benefit (firm, predictable stool formation) is the mechanism; the psychological benefit (permission to stop worrying and be present during intimacy) is the real purchase driver. Secondary problem: existing solutions either cause gas (inulin-based gummies), require swallowing 6–10 large capsules daily (Pure for Men), taste terrible (psyllium powder), or don't acknowledge the customer's actual use case (every mainstream brand).

**What objections do they have?** (required):
- "Is this actually different from Pure for Men, or just repackaged psyllium?" — Need to communicate the gummy format advantage + no-gas formulation clearly.
- "Will it cause gas or bloating? I've tried fibre before and it was awful." — This is the #1 objection. Must communicate psyllium vs. inulin difference and the FODMAP-free formula prominently.
- "Is it worth paying $28–$32 when I can buy Metamucil for $13?" — Need to justify the premium through explicit positioning, superior format, and community value.
- "Will it actually work for bottoming, or is it just a regular fibre supplement with gay branding?" — Need clinical dosing info, real testimonials, and transparent ingredient rationale.
- "I don't want something that tastes like medicine." — Taste is critical. The gummy format and flavour profile need to deliver on "actually enjoyable to eat."
- "Is it discreet enough? I don't want my housemates asking questions." — Packaging should be attractive and modern but not require explanation.

**How do they discover the brand?** (recommended):
Primarily through TikTok (#bottomtok, queer health creators, gay lifestyle content) and Instagram (targeted ads, influencer partnerships). Secondary discovery through Reddit (r/askgaybros, r/topsandbottoms — organic community discussion), Google search (queries like "best fibre for bottoming," "psyllium gummy for bottoming prep"), and word of mouth within friend groups and dating app conversations. Gay health influencers and queer dietitians/nurse practitioners are key amplifiers — the community already has trusted voices recommending fibre for bottoming prep; Downn needs to become the product those voices recommend.

## 4. The Competition

**Direct competitors** (required):
- **Pure for Men** ($54/240 capsules) — The category leader for explicit bottoming-positioned fibre. Identical core ingredient (psyllium + chia + flax + aloe). 91.3% five-star satisfaction in gay male segment. Downn's advantage: gummy format (solves the #1 complaint about Pure — pill burden of 6–10 large capsules/day), lower price (~40–50% cheaper), cleaner formula (no proprietary blend opacity).
- **Peachy Men** ($0.41–$0.68/gummy) — Emerging gummy competitor with explicit bottoming positioning and viral TikTok growth (4.7 stars, 43K+ reviews). Uses psyllium + prebiotic + probiotic. Downn's advantage: simpler, more transparent formula; potentially better price point; stronger clinical rationale.
- **Metamucil** (powder/capsules, $13–$15/month) — Dominates the "value" segment with 35–40% of the bottoming market through sheer cost advantage and mainstream trust. Zero gay positioning. Downn's advantage: explicit community positioning, gummy format, no powder-mixing hassle, speaks to the customer's actual use case.
- **Benefiber / FiberAdvance / Metamucil Gummies** ($10–$22) — Inulin-based gummies with zero bottoming community adoption despite mainstream popularity. Downn's advantage: psyllium (not inulin) = no gas. This is a biochemical advantage, not a marketing one — inulin causes fermentation and gas that directly contradicts bottoming prep.
- **Friend of Dorothy** ($25–$28/month, capsules) — UK-based, LGBTQ+ founder, explicit bottoming positioning. Growing but still niche. Capsule format. Downn's advantage: gummy format, US-first positioning, cleaner formula.

**What makes this brand different?** (required):
Downn is the only fibre gummy that combines all three things the bottoming community actually needs: psyllium-based (no gas), gummy format (no pill burden), and explicit sexual health positioning (no pretending this is about "digestive wellness"). Every other product gets one or two of these right but misses the third. Pure for Men nails the positioning and ingredients but is capsules. Peachy Men has the gummy format and positioning but is newer with less clinical rigour. Metamucil has the right ingredient but ignores the customer entirely. Inulin-based gummies have the format but the wrong chemistry. Downn closes all three gaps simultaneously.

**Aspirational brands** (recommended):
- **Pure for Men** — We admire how they built a brand identity around sexual confidence and community belonging, turning a commodity ingredient (psyllium) into a lifestyle product. Their "Stay Ready" messaging is effective. We want to match that emotional resonance while fixing the product experience (gummies > capsules, lower price).
- **Hims** — How they normalised taboo men's health topics (ED, hair loss) with clean design, direct language, and DTC convenience. The visual identity and tone feel premium without being clinical.
- **Athletic Greens (AG1)** — How they sell a single product with a long-form education-first page. One hero SKU, deep ingredient transparency, subscription-first model. Their "why each ingredient is here" approach matches our transparency value.
- **Ritual** — The "show exactly what's inside" visual approach (the transparent capsule). We want that same radical ingredient transparency applied to gummies.
